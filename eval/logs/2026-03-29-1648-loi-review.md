## Iteration Detail

### Iteration 1 — Industrial Lease Provisions
**Hypothesis:** Add an "Industrial Lease Provisions" section with manufacturing-specific LOI terms: power/electrical, environmental indemnification, chemical use, ventilation/exhaust, floor load, ITAR security, and industrial TI specifications.
**Target:** Industrial Specificity (3.0 → 7.0) + Requirement Capture (4.0 → 6.5)
**Delta:** +1.62 → KEPT
**What changed:** Added 8 industrial-specific LOI provisions: power/electrical (480V 3-phase, 800A+, inrush current), environmental provisions (Phase I ESA, pre-existing contamination indemnity), permitted use (manufacturing + chemical processing specifics), ventilation/exhaust (OSHA/EPA compliance), floor load (CNC machines 15,000+ lbs, slab warranty), chemical storage (landlord consent, fire code), ITAR/security (controlled access, landlord notice), and industrial TI specifics ($15-$80/SF cost ranges by category).

### Iteration 2 — Industrial Red Flags
**Hypothesis:** Expand the Red Flags section with industrial-specific LOI red flags: environmental liability, landlord restrictions on operations, power upgrade costs, insurance pass-through, and landlord approval delays.
**Target:** Red Flag Detection (5.0 → 8.0)
**Delta:** +0.63 → KEPT
**What changed:** Added 8 industrial-specific red flags: no environmental indemnification ($50K-$500K+ remediation), TI approval without timeline (manufacturing delay cost), power upgrade responsibility undefined ($50K-$200K), insurance cost pass-through for manufacturing (2-5x increase), permitted use clause too narrow, operating expense base year manipulation ($2-$5/SF surprises), assignment/subletting restrictions on 7-10 year leases, landlord access conflicting with ITAR requirements.

### Iteration 3 — Dealbreaker-First Review Sequence
**Hypothesis:** Add a "Dealbreaker Screen" at the top of the LOI review flow that checks the 5 terms that kill industrial deals before reviewing all other terms.

## Summary of Changes

The LOI review skill went from a generic commercial real estate LOI checklist (4.725) to an industrial-specific LOI negotiation engine (8.65). Key additions:

1. **Requirement Pre-Flight** — 10-item checklist that extracts industrial requirements before drafting, ensuring every operational need maps to a specific LOI term
2. **Dealbreaker Screen** — 5 front-loaded checks that kill bad deals before wasting time on the full review (permitted use, environmental, power, TI scope, timeline)
3. **Industrial Lease Provisions** — 8 manufacturing-specific LOI terms with cost ranges and technical specs (power, environmental, chemical, ventilation, floor load, ITAR, TI)
4. **Industrial Red Flags** — 8 industrial-specific red flags with dollar-value consequences (environmental remediation $50K-$500K+, transformer upgrades $50K-$200K, insurance 2-5x increase)
5. **Negotiation Benchmarks** — 9-term benchmark table with tenant-favorable/market/landlord-favorable ranges for industrial leases
6. **Example LOI Language** — Copy-paste provisions for permitted use, environmental indemnification, power/electrical, and TI approval timeline

The biggest single improvement was Iteration 1 (industrial lease provisions, +1.62), which transformed the skill from a generic CRE LOI checklist to an industrial-specific one. The skill's original structure (essential terms, terms people miss, drafting, negotiation) was sound — the gap was entirely in industrial depth and specificity.
