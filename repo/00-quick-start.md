## The 10 Things You Must Know Before You Start

**1. Industrial real estate is not like office or retail.**
Landlords are more sophisticated. Leases are more complex. The building specs that matter (clear height, dock doors, power) are non-negotiable after you sign.

**2. The broker works for the landlord — not you.**
In most deals, the listing broker represents the landlord. They're paid to maximize the landlord's outcome. Get your own broker or use this system to represent yourself effectively.

**3. NNN means you pay more than the rent.**
"Triple net" means you pay rent PLUS property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. In Houston, add $1.50–$2.50/SF to asking rent to get your true all-in cost.

**4. The asking rate is not the deal rate.**
Houston industrial deals are regularly closing 5–15% below asking. Know the market (see MARKET-CONTEXT.md) before you make your first offer.

**5. Clear height matters more than square footage.**
A 30,000 SF building at 18' clear is less usable than 25,000 SF at 28' clear for most operations. Know your actual minimum before you start.

**6. Your timeline determines your options.**
- Need space in 60 days → existing available inventory only
- Need space in 6 months → can consider spaces being vacated
- Need space in 12–18 months → can consider new construction
- Need space in 24+ months → can consider build-to-suit

**7. Buying vs. leasing is a real decision.**
At current cap rates (5.5–8.5% in Houston), many companies are better off buying. Run the math with Workflow 04 before you assume leasing is the answer.

**8. Incentives can be worth hundreds of thousands of dollars.**
Texas has property tax abatements, job creation credits, and sales tax exemptions for manufacturers. See `INCENTIVES.md` before you commit to a location. Read it before you commit to a county.

**9. The lease is forever. Negotiate every clause.**
Your landlord has a standard lease form that protects them at every turn. The clauses that will hurt you most: unlimited HVAC repair liability, restoration obligations, no subletting rights. See Workflow 07.

**10. You need a broker for the final 20%.**
This system gives you knowledge parity with professionals. But at the LOI and lease negotiation stage, having an experienced industrial broker on your side is worth the commission — which the landlord typically pays anyway.
