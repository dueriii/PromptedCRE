# Workflow 08 — Memo Output: Executive Summary for Stakeholders
> Version 1.0 | PromptedCRE Industrial Workflows

## What This Does
Produces a clean, one-page decision memo you can put in front of your CEO, CFO, board, or investment committee. Translates everything you've done in workflows 01–07 into a document designed to get a yes or an informed no.

---

## Prompt

```
You are an industrial real estate advisor. I've completed a full property evaluation and negotiation process. Produce a clean executive decision memo based on the information below.

[PASTE SUMMARIES FROM WORKFLOWS 01–07 OR DESCRIBE THE DEAL IN DETAIL]

Format as a professional one-page executive memo. Assume the reader is smart but has not been involved in the process. They need to understand the deal, the recommendation, the key risks, and what they're approving — in under 5 minutes.
```

## TIPS FOR A STRONG MEMO

**Lead with the recommendation.** Don't bury it. Decision-makers want to know what you're asking before they read the details.

**One number per section.** Too many numbers creates confusion. Pick the most important one.

**Address the objections you know they'll have.** If your CFO always asks about rent escalations, put it in the deal terms section. If your CEO always asks about the out clause, put it in key risks.

**Don't hedge.** "This could be a good deal" is useless. "This is the right deal because X" is what gets approved.

**The market context section is your armor.** When you show you know what comparable deals look like right now, your recommendation gains credibility.

## BONUS: LOI COVER EMAIL

```
Draft a brief, professional email to send alongside the LOI to [landlord name / seller name / their broker].

Tone: Direct and credible. We are a serious buyer/tenant with a real timeline. We've done our homework on the market and on this specific property. We want to move efficiently.

Include:
- Who we are (brief — 2 sentences)
- Why this property (1 sentence)
- Our timeline
- An invitation to discuss any questions before countering

Do not over-explain. Do not apologize for our terms. Do not say "we're very excited."
```
