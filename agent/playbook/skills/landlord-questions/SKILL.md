# Landlord Questions — Surface Problems, Build Leverage

Generate targeted questions for the landlord, listing broker, or seller that reveal the real condition of the property and the motivation behind the deal.

## When This Activates

- User is about to talk to a landlord or their broker
- User asks "what should I ask them?"
- User has toured and wants follow-up questions
- User is in negotiation and needs leverage-building questions

## Philosophy

Good questions do two things:
1. **Protect you** — surface problems before you sign
2. **Build leverage** — reveal how motivated the other side is

Never ask questions you can answer yourself (public records, listing data). Ask questions only the landlord/seller knows.

## Question Bank by Category

### Industrial Building Systems & Infrastructure
1. What is the electrical service? Specifically: voltage, phase, total amperage, and number/size of panels?
2. What is the transformer capacity (kVA)? Is it owned by the landlord or the utility? When was it last upgraded?
3. Is there capacity for additional electrical load, or would a transformer/service upgrade be required?
4. What is the clear height (floor to bottom of joist/truss)?
5. What is the slab thickness and floor load capacity (PSF)?
6. What type of fire suppression is installed — ESFR, wet, dry, or foam? What density/design is it rated for?
7. Is there compressed air infrastructure? What capacity (CFM/PSI) and how many drops?
8. Are there floor drains? Where do they connect — sanitary sewer, storm, or holding tank?
9. Does the building have industrial ventilation or exhaust systems? What capacity?
10. What is the column spacing? Are there any interior columns that restrict clear span?
11. Is there crane infrastructure (bridge crane, jib crane)? What capacity?
12. What is the dock configuration — how many dock-high doors, grade-level doors, and what are the dock leveler ratings?
13. What is the truck court depth? Can the yard accommodate 53' trailers for full turns?

### Manufacturing & Chemical Process (when tenant does production, surface treatment, food, chemical handling)
14. Does the building have an existing industrial wastewater discharge permit? What are the permitted discharge limits?
15. Are there restrictions on chemical storage type or quantity (from landlord, insurance, or municipality)?
16. Has the building previously been used for chemical processing or manufacturing with hazardous materials?
17. What is the building's security infrastructure — controlled access points, fencing, camera systems? (Critical for ITAR/DPAS-rated facilities)
18. Are there isolated/dedicated electrical circuits available for high-inrush equipment (CNC machines, surface treatment lines)?
19. Is the HVAC zoned separately for office vs. production areas? Is there makeup air for exhaust systems?
20. What is the landlord's process for approving tenant modifications — especially chemical containment, ventilation penetrations, and slab modifications?

### Lease Gotchas (questions tenants forget to ask)
21. Are there any exclusivity clauses that restrict what tenants can operate in the building or park?
22. Does the lease include a landlord's right to relocate the tenant?
23. What are the assignment and subletting restrictions? Can we assign the lease if we sell the business?
24. Is there a demolition or redevelopment clause that could terminate the lease early?
25. What is the base year for operating expenses, and how are increases calculated?
26. Are there any co-tenancy requirements or restrictions from other tenants in the park?

### Building Condition & History
14. When was the roof last replaced? Is there an active warranty?
15. What's the age and condition of the HVAC systems?
16. Have there been any insurance claims on the property in the last 5 years?
17. Is there any deferred maintenance we should know about?
18. Has the property ever had water intrusion, flooding, or mold issues?
19. When was the electrical system last upgraded?
20. Are there any known foundation or structural issues?

### Environmental
21. Has a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment been completed? When?
22. Were any Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs) identified?
23. Has a Phase II ever been triggered? Results?
24. Any underground storage tanks (active or decommissioned)?
25. What were the previous uses of the property?
26. Is the property in a flood plain? FEMA zone?

### Lease / Financial (for leasing)
27. What are the actual NNN expenses? Get the last 12 months of CAM reconciliation.
28. What's the property tax bill? Any pending reassessment?
29. What's the insurance cost? Has it increased recently?
30. Is the landlord willing to provide a TI allowance? How much?
31. What's the landlord's standard lease form? Can we see it before submitting an LOI?
32. Are there any existing tenants? What are their lease terms?
33. Who manages the property? On-site or remote?
34. What capital improvements has the landlord made in the last 3 years?

### Sale / Acquisition (for purchasing)
35. Why is the owner selling?
36. How long has the property been on the market?
37. Have there been any prior offers? Why didn't they close?
38. What's the current NOI? Can we get a trailing-12 P&L?
39. Are there any existing leases or tenants that convey with the sale?
40. What's the property tax assessed value vs. asking price?
41. Any pending or threatened litigation involving the property?
42. Are there any easements, encumbrances, or title issues?

### Zoning & Use
43. Current zoning? Any variance or special use permits?
44. Any restrictions on hours of operation, noise, or truck traffic?
45. Are there any deed restrictions or HOA/property association rules?
46. Can we confirm permitted uses with the municipality?
47. Any upcoming zoning changes or municipal projects that could affect the property?

### Market & Motivation (leverage-building)
48. How long has this space been vacant?
49. What's the landlord's occupancy rate across their portfolio?
50. Are there other offers currently being considered?
51. What's the landlord's ideal timeline for execution?
52. Is the landlord open to creative structures (free rent, stepped rent, early termination options)?
53. Has the asking rate been adjusted since listing? How many times?

### Expansion & Future
54. Is there adjacent space available for future expansion?
55. Does the landlord own adjacent land? Could they build additional space?
56. Are there any planned developments nearby that could affect the property?
57. What are the landlord's long-term plans for the property/portfolio?

## How to Use These

Don't send all questions. Select 8–12 and **sequence them by priority tier**:

### Tier 1 — Dealbreakers (ask these first)
Questions where a bad answer means the building doesn't work, regardless of price. If any Tier 1 answer is a no-go, stop — don't waste time on Tier 2 and 3.

For industrial tenants, common dealbreakers are:
- Power capacity insufficient and upgrade timeline exceeds their move-in date
- Zoning doesn't allow their specific use (chemical processing, heavy manufacturing)
- Environmental contamination from prior tenants
- Slab too thin for heavy equipment (cannot be fixed)
- No path to required permitting (wastewater discharge, fume exhaust)

### Tier 2 — Cost Drivers (ask these next)
Questions where a bad answer means significant unbudgeted expense, but the building could still work if the cost is manageable or the landlord contributes:
- Fire suppression upgrade needed for commodity type
- Transformer upgrade cost and who pays
- Roof age and NNN replacement responsibility
- TI allowance and buildout scope
- Insurance cost impact from manufacturing use

### Tier 3 — Leverage Builders (ask last)
Questions that reveal the landlord's motivation and create negotiating leverage:
- Vacancy duration, portfolio occupancy, rate adjustments
- Other offers, timeline pressure, creative deal structures

### Selection Criteria
Also consider:
- The user's specific situation (lease vs. buy)
- What they already know from the tour
- **What gaps exist in the Property Card** — check memory.md for open questions from property-survey and tour-prep
- **What the comparison flagged** — prioritize questions that address risks or unknowns identified in the comparison

Present questions in tier order with a note on why each matters and what a bad answer looks like.

## Red Flags in Answers

Coach the user on what to watch for:

### General Red Flags
- **Vague answers about roof/HVAC age** → probably old, probably expensive
- **"We've never had any issues"** → they might not know, or they're not disclosing
- **Reluctance to share financials** → could be hiding vacancy or expense problems
- **"We have other offers"** → pressure tactic, often exaggerated. Ask for specifics.
- **Long vacancy + no price reduction** → unrealistic landlord, room to negotiate hard

### Industrial-Specific Red Flags (the expensive ones)
- **Transformer is utility-owned or undersized** → upgrade costs $50K–$200K and takes 6–12 months. This is a potential dealbreaker for manufacturing tenants. Ask who pays.
- **Fire suppression is standard wet/dry but tenant stores chemicals or changes commodity type** → upgrading to ESFR or foam can cost $5–$15/SF. Landlords often push this cost to the tenant.
- **No Phase I ESA or ESA is more than 6 months old** → always required for industrial. Prior tenants' contamination becomes your liability if not documented. Phase II remediation can cost $100K+.
- **Vague on slab thickness or says "standard"** → older industrial buildings may have 4" slabs that cannot support heavy CNC, presses, or concentrated racking loads. Verify — you cannot fix a slab.
- **Floor drains connect to storm sewer** → industrial discharge (chemicals, coolants, washdown) into storm drains triggers EPA violations. Must connect to sanitary sewer with proper permitting.
- **NNN lease but roof is 15+ years old** → tenant is responsible for roof replacement in most NNN structures. A 30K SF roof replacement costs $150K–$250K. Negotiate a cap or landlord responsibility.
- **CAM reconciliation shows 10%+ increase year-over-year** → landlord may be shifting capital expenses into operating expenses. Request 3 years of reconciliations.
- **Insurance cost "depends on your use"** → manufacturing and chemical storage significantly increase property insurance. Get a quote from your insurer BEFORE signing. Chemical handling can 2–3× the premium.
- **Landlord hesitant about chemical use or ITAR tenants** → some landlords won't accept the liability. Better to know now than after LOI.

## Output Format

Produce two outputs:

### 1. Strategy Brief (for the user)
For each selected question, include:
- The question
- **Why it matters** for their specific operation
- **Red flag answer** — what response should concern them
- **Priority tier** (1/2/3)

### 2. Email-Ready Question List (to send to listing broker)
A professional, concise list the user can copy-paste into an email to the listing broker or landlord. Format:

> Subject: Information Request — [Property Address]
>
> We're evaluating [address] for [company] and have the following questions. Answers to these will help us move quickly to an LOI if the building is a fit.
>
> **Building Systems**
> 1. [Question]
> 2. [Question]
>
> **Environmental / Zoning**
> 3. [Question]
>
> **Lease Terms**
> 4. [Question]
>
> Please let us know if any of these are easier to address on a call. We're available [user's availability].

Strip out the strategy notes — the listing broker shouldn't see your red flag analysis or leverage-building intent. The email should read as professional and direct.

## After Answers Are Received

Update memory with key findings. Update the Property Card with new information and revised dimension scores.

## Transition

- After questions are answered → update Property Card, re-evaluate
- If answers reveal concerns that need professional inspection → `due-diligence`
- If ready to make an offer → `loi-review`
- If comparing updated info → `comparison`
