# Tour Prep — Before You Walk the Building

Prepare the user to get maximum value from a property tour.

## When This Activates

- User says they're going to visit / tour a building
- User has selected a shortlist and wants to see them in person
- User asks "what should I look for?"

## Before Generating the Checklist

Check memory.md (or ask the user) for an existing Property Card for this building. If one exists, pull out:
- **Open questions** from the property survey — these become priority items to investigate on-site
- **Gaps in specs** — things the listing didn't specify that you need to verify in person
- **Red flags** — anything concerning from the initial evaluation

If no Property Card exists, note which specs are unknown and flag them in the observation guide.

## Pre-Tour Checklist

### Before You Go
- [ ] Confirm tour date/time with listing broker or landlord
- [ ] Get the listing broker's cell phone (in case you can't find the building)
- [ ] Download or print the property flyer / listing sheet
- [ ] **Review your Property Card** for this building (from property-survey) — bring the open questions list
- [ ] **Print or save the comparison matrix** if comparing multiple buildings on the same tour day
- [ ] Bring a tape measure or laser measurer
- [ ] Wear closed-toe shoes (warehouse floors, loading docks)
- [ ] Bring your phone — take photos of EVERYTHING

### On-Site Observation Guide

Walk through these in order. **Start with dealbreakers** — don't spend 45 minutes on a full walkthrough if the building fails on a non-negotiable.

#### Dealbreaker Check (First 5 Minutes)

Before the full walkthrough, verify the specs that kill the deal. Pull these from your Property Card's must-haves:

- [ ] **Power:** Walk to the main electrical panel first. If the service amperage or voltage is wrong and there's no transformer upgrade path, stop here. Transformer upgrades cost $50K-$200K and take 6-12 months.
- [ ] **Clear height:** Quick visual or tape measure check. If the listing said 28' and it's actually 24', this may not work.
- [ ] **Zoning confirmation:** Ask the tour guide to confirm the permitted uses — especially if your operation involves chemicals, heavy manufacturing, or processes that many industrial parks restrict.
- [ ] **Obvious environmental red flags:** Stained floors, chemical odors, underground storage tank markers, monitoring wells in the parking lot. Any of these means expensive due diligence before you can proceed.

If any dealbreaker fails: note it, ask your questions for reference, and move on to your next building. Don't waste time scoring a building that can't work.

#### Exterior (5-10 min)
- [ ] Truck court: Can a 53' trailer turn around? How deep?
- [ ] Loading doors: Count dock-high and grade-level. Condition of levelers and seals.
- [ ] Yard / outside storage: Fenced? Paved or gravel? Drainage?
- [ ] Parking: Count spaces. Condition of lot. Employee vs. visitor.
- [ ] Roof: Age? Material? Any visible patches, ponding, or equipment?
- [ ] Signage: Visibility from the road?
- [ ] Neighboring uses: Anything concerning? (chemicals, heavy traffic, noise)

#### Interior — Warehouse (10-15 min)
- [ ] Clear height: Does it match the listing? Measure if possible.
- [ ] Column spacing: Walk the grid. Will your racking / equipment fit?
- [ ] Floor condition: Cracks, joints, levelness? Sealed or unsealed?
- [ ] **Floor slab thickness:** Ask or check records — 4" slabs can't support heavy CNC equipment (15,000+ lbs). Manufacturing needs 6" minimum. Older buildings often have thinner slabs than expected.
- [ ] Lighting: Type (LED, fluorescent, HID)? Adequate for your operations?
- [ ] **Power — read the panel closely:**
  - Voltage and phase (480V 3-phase is standard for manufacturing)
  - Main breaker amperage — is it enough for your equipment load plus inrush current?
  - Panel capacity — how many open breaker slots for future circuits?
  - Transformer location and capacity (outside or utility pad) — take a photo of the nameplate
- [ ] Fire suppression: Sprinkler type? ESFR? Adequate for your inventory/commodity type? Chemical storage may require specialized suppression beyond standard ESFR.
- [ ] Crane: If present — type, capacity, span, runway condition
- [ ] HVAC: Warehouse heated? Cooled? Fans? Adequate ventilation?
- [ ] Restrooms: Count, condition, ADA compliance

#### Interior — Manufacturing & Process Infrastructure (if applicable)
- [ ] **Floor drains:** Present? Where do they connect — sanitary sewer or storm drain? Surface treatment, wash-down, and chemical processes require drains with proper industrial discharge permitting.
- [ ] **Compressed air:** Existing compressor and piping? Count drops. Manufacturing/CNC operations typically need 100+ CFM at 90 PSI.
- [ ] **Ventilation & exhaust:** Dedicated fume extraction for chemical processes (plating, coating, anodizing)? Ductwork condition? OSHA/EPA compliant? Look for existing hood systems, roof exhausts, make-up air units.
- [ ] **Chemical storage areas:** Containment berms or secondary containment? Adequate separation from other operations? Proper ventilation?
- [ ] **Wastewater:** Any existing wastewater pretreatment system? Discharge permits in place from prior tenants?

#### Interior — Office (5 min)
- [ ] Layout: Private offices vs. open plan? Fits your team?
- [ ] Condition: Move-in ready or needs renovation?
- [ ] Restrooms, break room, conference room
- [ ] IT: Data wiring, server room, internet access points

### Photos to Take
1. Every loading door (inside and out)
2. Electrical panel (close-up of specs)
3. Roof condition (from ground + interior underside)
4. Column spacing (stand at one column, photo toward the next)
5. Floor condition (especially any cracks or damage)
6. HVAC equipment
7. Truck court (wide shot showing turning radius)
8. Office areas
9. Any damage, deferred maintenance, or concerns
10. Exterior / street view (for your team back at the office)

### Questions to Ask the Tour Guide

**Building & Systems:**
- What's the age of the roof? Last replaced? Any warranty remaining?
- What's the electrical service? (Voltage, phase, amperage.) Is the transformer dedicated or shared? Any capacity for upgrade?
- What's the HVAC capacity? Age of units?
- What's the slab thickness? Has it been tested or cored?
- Any environmental issues? Phase I/II done? What did the prior tenant(s) operate here?

**Manufacturing & Process (if applicable):**
- Are there existing floor drains? Where do they discharge — sanitary or storm?
- Does the building have industrial discharge permits in place?
- Any restrictions on chemical storage, hazardous materials, or manufacturing processes?
- What's the fire suppression system rated for? What commodity class?
- Is there an existing compressed air system? Capacity?

**Deal & Lease:**
- Who pays for what in NNN? Get the CAM/tax/insurance breakdown. What were actual NNN costs last year?
- What's the landlord's TI budget? Willing to build out? Or provide allowance?
- How long has it been vacant? Why did the last tenant leave?
- Any exclusivity clauses, use restrictions, or operating hour limits?
- Does the landlord have a right to relocate tenants?
- What's the assignment/subletting policy?
- What other offers are you seeing?
- What's the landlord's timeline — how motivated are they?

### Expensive Surprises to Watch For

These are the things that don't show up on listings but cost real money. Note any that apply during your walkthrough:

- [ ] **Transformer capacity:** If the existing transformer can't handle your load, budget $50K-$200K and 6-12 months for an upgrade. Check the nameplate — if it's undersized, this is a lease negotiation item (who pays?).
- [ ] **Fire suppression mismatch:** Standard ESFR may not cover your commodity type. If you're storing chemicals, flammables, or high-piled stock, the system may need upgrading ($5-$15/SF). Ask what the prior tenant stored.
- [ ] **Roof condition + NNN responsibility:** In NNN leases, YOU may pay for roof repairs. If the roof is 15+ years old, you're inheriting a ticking clock. Budget $4-$8/SF for replacement. Get the roof warranty and inspection report.
- [ ] **Environmental history:** What did prior tenants do here? Chemical manufacturing, dry cleaning, metal finishing, fuel storage — all leave contamination. Phase I ESA is always required, but ask the tour guide directly. Look for monitoring wells, stained concrete, chemical odors.
- [ ] **ITAR / security requirements:** If your operation requires controlled access (ITAR, NIST 800-171, classified work), evaluate the building's physical security: perimeter fencing, access control points, separate entrances for visitors vs. employees, secure storage areas. Retrofitting security is expensive.
- [ ] **Insurance implications:** Manufacturing processes, chemical storage, and spray operations significantly increase insurance costs. Ask the landlord if there are restrictions on chemical quantities or processes.

## After the Tour

Rate the property on your three dimensions (Location / Pricing / Functionality) based on what you saw. Update the Property Card with your observations.

If touring multiple buildings, keep notes fresh — tour fatigue causes you to blend buildings together. Write your top impression of each building within 30 minutes of leaving.

## After the Tour — Memory Update

Update memory with tour observations: what stood out, what concerned you, and your updated dimension scores.
