## Agent's Negotiation Strategy Notes

**BATNA:** Fallbrook Dr is eliminated on functionality, but landlord doesn't know that. Reference "other options" without naming them.

**Leverage points:**
1. Building has been vacant 6 months — landlord is losing $78K/month in potential rent
2. 7-year term is attractive — reduces landlord's re-leasing risk
3. Apex is a credit-worthy ITAR manufacturer — low-risk tenant
4. Manufacturing buildout increases building value for future tenants

**Expect pushback on:**
1. **Base rent** — likely counter at $12.50-13.00. Target settlement: $12.00-12.25.
2. **TI allowance** — $15/SF is aggressive. Expect counter at $7-8/SF. Target: $10-12/SF.
3. **Crane as landlord work** — may counter with increased TI instead. Either works.
4. **Early termination** — landlord may reject entirely. Consider dropping if you get strong TI and rate.

**Package strategy:** If landlord pushes back on rate, trade for: "We'll accept $12.50 if you provide $12/SF TI, include the crane as landlord work, and give us 4 months free rent." Bundle concessions — don't negotiate each term in isolation.
