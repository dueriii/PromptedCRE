# Landlord Questions — Surface Problems, Build Leverage

Generate targeted questions for the landlord, listing broker, or seller that reveal the real condition of the property and the motivation behind the deal.

## When This Activates

- User is about to talk to a landlord or their broker
- User asks "what should I ask them?"
- User has toured and wants follow-up questions
- User is in negotiation and needs leverage-building questions

## Philosophy

Good questions do two things:
1. **Protect you** — surface problems before you sign
2. **Build leverage** — reveal how motivated the other side is

Never ask questions you can answer yourself (public records, listing data). Ask questions only the landlord/seller knows.

## Question Bank by Category

### Building Condition & History
1. When was the roof last replaced? Is there an active warranty?
2. What's the age and condition of the HVAC systems?
3. Have there been any insurance claims on the property in the last 5 years?
4. Is there any deferred maintenance we should know about?
5. Has the property ever had water intrusion, flooding, or mold issues?
6. What's the condition of the fire suppression system? Last inspection date?
7. When was the electrical system last upgraded?
8. Are there any known foundation or structural issues?

### Environmental
9. Has a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment been completed? When?
10. Were any Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs) identified?
11. Has a Phase II ever been triggered? Results?
12. Any underground storage tanks (active or decommissioned)?
13. What were the previous uses of the property?
14. Is the property in a flood plain? FEMA zone?

### Lease / Financial (for leasing)
15. What are the actual NNN expenses? Get the last 12 months of CAM reconciliation.
16. What's the property tax bill? Any pending reassessment?
17. What's the insurance cost? Has it increased recently?
18. Is the landlord willing to provide a TI allowance? How much?
19. What's the landlord's standard lease form? Can we see it before submitting an LOI?
20. Are there any existing tenants? What are their lease terms?
21. Who manages the property? On-site or remote?
22. What capital improvements has the landlord made in the last 3 years?

### Sale / Acquisition (for purchasing)
23. Why is the owner selling?
24. How long has the property been on the market?
25. Have there been any prior offers? Why didn't they close?
26. What's the current NOI? Can we get a trailing-12 P&L?
27. Are there any existing leases or tenants that convey with the sale?
28. What's the property tax assessed value vs. asking price?
29. Any pending or threatened litigation involving the property?
30. Are there any easements, encumbrances, or title issues?

### Zoning & Use
31. Current zoning? Any variance or special use permits?
32. Any restrictions on hours of operation, noise, or truck traffic?
33. Are there any deed restrictions or HOA/property association rules?
34. Can we confirm permitted uses with the municipality?
35. Any upcoming zoning changes or municipal projects that could affect the property?

### Market & Motivation (leverage-building)
36. How long has this space been vacant?
37. What's the landlord's occupancy rate across their portfolio?
38. Are there other offers currently being considered?
39. What's the landlord's ideal timeline for execution?
40. Is the landlord open to creative structures (free rent, stepped rent, early termination options)?
41. Has the asking rate been adjusted since listing? How many times?

### Expansion & Future
42. Is there adjacent space available for future expansion?
43. Does the landlord own adjacent land? Could they build additional space?
44. Are there any planned developments nearby that could affect the property?
45. What are the landlord's long-term plans for the property/portfolio?

## How to Use These

Don't send all 45 questions. Select the 8-12 most relevant based on:
- The user's specific situation (lease vs. buy)
- What they already know from the tour
- **What gaps exist in the Property Card** — check memory.md (or ask the user) for open questions from property-survey and tour-prep observations
- Where they suspect issues
- **What the comparison flagged** — if a comparison identified specific risks or unknowns for this property, prioritize questions that address those gaps

Present them in a prioritized list with a note on why each question matters.

## Red Flags in Answers

Coach the user on what to watch for:
- **Vague answers about roof/HVAC age** → probably old, probably expensive
- **"We've never had any issues"** → they might not know, or they're not disclosing
- **Reluctance to share financials** → could be hiding vacancy or expense problems
- **"We have other offers"** → pressure tactic, often exaggerated. Ask for specifics.
- **Long vacancy + no price reduction** → unrealistic landlord, room to negotiate hard

## After Answers Are Received

Update memory with key findings. Update the Property Card with new information and revised dimension scores.

## Transition

- After questions are answered → update Property Card, re-evaluate
- If answers reveal concerns that need professional inspection → `due-diligence`
- If ready to make an offer → `loi-review`
- If comparing updated info → `comparison`
